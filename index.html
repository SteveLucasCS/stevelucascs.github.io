From homeland security, to economics, then mechanical engineering, I finally ended up in the expansive field computer science. Through all of my interests, there have been important constants, which define who I am today. My biggest passion in life is learning, and I believe my biggest strength is my growth mindset. I thrive when I can be creative and solve problems; a job that engages my brain is a must for me. When I have a challenge to overcome or an opportunity exercise my creativity, I have an incredible drive and determination. Engaging tasks are not"work" to me; creative problem solving is the most fulfilling activity in my life. When I get into a project, I will not stop until I am satisfied that my work is as perfect as I can make it, and better than the work I did the day before. I strive to go above and beyond, often asking "why not?" instead of "why." I crave a deep understanding of the world around me, which leads to me studying topics such as astronomy, quantum physics, and economics in my spare time. Luckily, I also have more practical hobbies, such as hockey and dogs.

I am lucky to be an ambitious dreamer; I will always have a drive to be a better person than the day before. My ideal career would be developing innovative technologies that could change the world. I often ponder what the future holds, especially in the ways of technology. My dream is to help create devices that would benefit mankind, and I believe there are many opportunities emerging that can make that possible. With ideas such as in-home MRI machines, to the internet of things, artificial intelligence, human-machine integration, and countless more we cannot even fathom right now, I believe a new technological era is just around the corner. My goal is to be part of it, and following my ambition is how I plan to get there.
